Meet your guide
Dexter Cohen Bohn Gallery Director
NDINA Gallery · Fine Art of Fiji

My name is Dexter Cohen Bohn. Cohen comes from my great-grandfather Hymie, a Yiddish speaking printmaker born in 1886 near Mykolaiev in Southern Ukraine. At the age of 20, persecution uprooted Hymie and 2 million others in his generation. Arriving in Boston with only a small suitcase he built an empowering press that gave voice to labour unions, suffragists, and underground justice movements. His art was a bridge for other people's truth into the world.

Growing up I fit in but never really knew where I belonged — my roots grew in confusing trans-Atlantic ways eventually leading to grassroots social activism in Bangkok. I drifted here and there without being able to answer the simplest question: where are you from? Then a friendly stranger came up to me on a packed train and asked in a Bostonian accent 'do you drink kava?' Two days later I discovered a kalapu (Tongan for kava club) and a communal practice of heartfelt exchange that grounded me like nothing else ever had.

"When you are rooted in your ancestral story, you have a star compass that will guide you home — no matter how lost you may feel."

Not content with second-hand stories, I moved to Fiji seeking to be immersed in kava culture. It wasn't mellow. In the hardest stretch of a bureaucratic tailspin, my only lifeline was a small cadre of wildly talented artists who shared their kava and stories with me every night in a thatch hut. They taught me that art — real, rooted art — is how we remember and embody who we are and where we are from.

Those artists wish the world to hear their truth. So we built a new vessel for cross-cultural exchange: NDINA — a gallery bringing the fine art of Fiji to the world. Inspired by my great-grandfather who found himself in a new land and used his press to be of service — at NDINA you'll discover works and wisdom rooted in authentic relationship to vanua (Vosa vakaviti for land, sea, sky, people) that will directly empower the torchbearers of truth.

This masterclass will launch our canoe — our gallery — into the world. It is a chance for you to climb aboard our maiden voyage, learn with our talented crew of artists, and be the first wind in our sails.

Honest disclosure

This course is not for everyone.

This is not for those who are intensely traumatized by their ancestral story and not yet ready to encounter it in art form.

This is an artistic voyage into your ancestry — it will not be only beautiful. You will encounter the suffering of your ancestors. Those tears will go into your art. This course will require you to speak with your living family elders.

If you are ready for that voyage, there is a seat at the kava bowl with your name on it.
